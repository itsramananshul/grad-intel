# Gradintel — Your Academic Performance Dashboard

**Track your grades, predict your GPA, and never get surprised by your results again.**

Gradintel is a free web app for university students. You log your exam scores, and it tells you your current GPA, what you need to score on upcoming exams to hit your grade targets, and gives you AI-powered study advice — all in one place.

---

## Getting Started

### Creating an Account

1. Go to the Gradintel website
2. Click **Create Account**
3. Enter your full name, email, password, and university name
4. Click **Create Account →**
5. Check your email and confirm your account if prompted
6. Sign back in and you're ready to go

Your data is saved to the cloud automatically — you can log in from any device and your grades will be there.

## The Sidebar

- **Main** — Dashboard, Add Subject, My Courses, Semesters

## Adding Your First Subject

This is the most important thing to learn. Everything else in the app flows from this.

1. Click **Add Subject** in the sidebar
2. **Step 1 — Choose your mode:**
   - **Single Subject** — adding one course
   - **Multi-Subject** — adding several courses at once
3. **Step 2 — Fill in your subject details:**
   - Enter the subject name (e.g. "Calculus II")
   - Add each exam component with its name, weight (%), and max marks. For example: Midterm (30%, max 100), Final (50%, max 100), Assignments (20%, max 50)
   - Weights must add up to 100%
4. **Step 3 — Enter your scores:**
   - For exams you've already taken, enter your score
   - Leave future exams blank
5. Click **Calculate**

You'll instantly see your current grade, your predicted final grade, and exactly what you need on each remaining exam to finish with an A, B, or C.

> **Tip:** Come back and update your scores any time you get results back.

## Dashboard

After adding your subjects, the Dashboard is your home screen.

- **GPA / Semester / Subjects / Credits** at the very top — a quick summary visible on every page
- **Stat cards** — your current score, predicted final score, subject count, and total credits
- **Goal Ring** — circular progress showing how close you are to your target GPA (set your target in Policies)
- **At-Risk subjects** — if any course is in danger of hurting your GPA, it shows here in red with a warning
- **GPA Trend chart** — a line chart of your scores across all exams this semester so you can see if you're improving or declining

## My Courses

A full list of all subjects in your current semester.

- Click any subject to open its **full detail view**, which shows:
  - Your current score with a large animated number at the top
  - A breakdown of every exam — your score, the letter grade, and GPA points
  - **Scores Needed** — exactly what you need on each remaining exam for every possible final grade (A through F)
  - A score chart showing all your exam results visually
  - A **Share Card** — generates a card you can screenshot and send to friends showing your grade

To edit a subject, click the edit ✏️ icon next to it in the list. To delete, click the trash 🗑️ icon.

## Semesters

Every semester you complete gets saved here permanently.

- Click any past semester to expand it and see all subjects and grades from that term
- Once you have two or more semesters, a **GPA Trend chart** appears showing your academic trajectory over time
- To start a new semester, go to **Add Subject** and click **New Semester** at the top — your old semester stays in history

## Analytics

Deep performance insights beyond just your GPA number.

- **Subject DNA** — a spider/radar chart showing your strengths and weaknesses across all subjects
- **Semester Delta** — compares this semester to your previous one. Green means improvement, red means decline
- **Performance Heatmap** — a color-coded grid of all your exams. Darker = higher score. Great for spotting patterns
- **Best Subjects** — your top 5 performing courses ranked
- **Needs Attention** — courses where your score is below your goal
- **Grade Distribution** — a bar chart showing how many A's, B's, C's you currently have
- **Danger Zone Tracker** — courses where one bad exam could seriously hurt your final grade

## What-If Simulator

Answers the question: *"What if I get X on my final?"*

**Quick What-If:** Pick a subject and drag the slider to see how different exam scores change your final grade in real time. Great for deciding how much you need to study before an exam.

**GPA Time Machine:** Drag a slider that applies a percentage change across all your subjects at once. Shows your actual GPA vs what it would be if you scored higher or lower across the board.

## Score Lab

## Planner

Your deadline and task manager, built into the same app as your grades.

**Adding a deadline:**
1. Click the 📌 floating button in the top-right of any page, or go to **Planner** in the sidebar
2. Click **+ Add Deadline**
3. Fill in the title, due date, course, weight %, and type (Assignment, Exam, Quiz, Project)
4. Click **Save Deadline**

**Reading your deadline list:**
- 🔴 Red = overdue
- 🟡 Yellow = due within 48 hours
- Normal = upcoming
- Click ✓ to mark something done
- Filter by All / Upcoming / Overdue using the chips at the top

If you use Canvas LMS, deadlines can be imported automatically — see the Canvas Sync section.

## Study Planner

Tools for managing your actual study time.

**Study Time Estimator:** Shows how many hours you need per subject based on what score you need and how heavily that exam is weighted. The subject with the ★ star is your highest ROI — studying that one gives you the biggest grade improvement per hour spent.

**Exam Calendar:** Add specific dates to each exam, then click **Export .ics Calendar** to download a file you can add to Google Calendar, Apple Calendar, or Outlook.

**Pomodoro Timer:**
1. Pick a course from the dropdown
2. Click Start — 25 minutes work, 5 minutes break
3. Each session is logged under that course so you can track hours studied per subject

**Activity Streak:** A GitHub-style calendar heatmap showing which days you used the app. Your current consecutive streak is shown below it.

**Mood Log:** After studying, tap how you're feeling (😄 to 😞). The app tracks your mood over time so you can see if stress is affecting your grades.

**Course Notes:** A private notepad attached to each course. Use it for anything you want to remember — professor tips, topics to review, key dates.

## AI Assistant

Ask the AI anything about your grades and it responds based on your actual data.

**Example questions:**
- *"I have 3 exams left. Midterm was 30% and I got 72. What do I need for a B?"*
- *"Which subject should I focus on this week?"*
- *"Make me a 2-week study plan for finals"*
- *"I have Calculus and Physics on the same day — how do I split my time?"*

The AI already knows your courses and current scores — you don't need to repeat your grades in every message.

**Free AI (no setup needed):** Click the 🤖 button floating in the bottom-right corner of any page for a quick chat. Uses a free AI with no API key required.

**Better AI responses (optional):**
1. In the AI Assistant tab, click **Use own key**
2. Choose a provider — **Groq is recommended, it's completely free**
3. Click the link to get your free key (takes 2 minutes on their website)
4. Paste the key in and save

Your key is stored only in your own browser — it is never shared with anyone except the AI provider you chose.

**Syllabus Upload:** Click **Upload Syllabus** in the AI Assistant tab and drop in your course PDF. The AI reads it and automatically extracts subject names, exam weights, and important dates into your tracker.

## Degree Tracker

Track your progress toward actually graduating.

2. Click **+ Add Degree** and enter your degree name

## Canvas Sync

If your university uses Canvas LMS, you can import grades directly instead of typing them in manually.

**Method 1 — CSV Upload:**
1. In Canvas, open a course → click **Grades** → download the CSV file
2. In Gradintel, go to **Canvas Sync** → choose **Upload CSV**
3. Upload your file, review the preview, click **Import**

**Method 2 — Bookmarklet (for live sync):**
1. Go to **Canvas Sync** → choose **Bookmarklet**
2. Drag the purple **"Gradintel Assignments"** button to your browser's bookmarks bar
3. Navigate to any Canvas course page
4. Click the bookmarklet — it reads your grades and sends them to Gradintel instantly
5. Use **Sync ALL Courses** to grab every course at once

Assignment due dates from Canvas are automatically sent to your Planner when you use the bookmarklet.

## Policies

Customize how Gradintel calculates your GPA to match your university's specific rules.

- **GPA Scale** — switch between 4.0, 4.3, or 5.0
- **Grade Replacement** — if your university lets you retake courses and replace the grade
- **Pass/No Pass** — toggle so P/NP courses don't count toward your GPA
- **Withdrawal** — whether W grades count as F or are excluded
- **Weighted GPA** — for universities that use weighted calculations
- **Goal GPA** — set this and the Dashboard highlights how close you are, flags at-risk subjects, and unlocks achievements when you hit it
- **Goal Credits** — set how many total credits you're aiming for

## Common Questions

**Q: Will my grades be deleted if I clear my browser?**
No. Everything is saved to the cloud. As long as you're signed in, your data is safe from any device.

**Q: Can I use this on my phone?**
Yes. The app works on mobile browsers. Tap ☰ in the top-left corner to open the sidebar.

**Q: My exam weights don't add up to 100% — is that okay?**
No, they need to add up to exactly 100% for the GPA calculation to be accurate. Edit the subject and fix the weights.

**Q: My actual grade is different from what Gradintel predicted — why?**
Predictions are only as accurate as the weights you entered. If your grade is off, either a weight was slightly wrong or your professor applied a curve. Update the score in My Courses and it recalculates instantly.

**Q: How do I start a new semester?**
Go to **Add Subject** → click **New Semester** at the top. Your old semester stays in history.

**Q: Can I delete a subject?**
Yes. Go to **My Courses** → hover the subject → click the trash icon. This permanently removes it from that semester.

**Q: The AI doesn't seem to know my grades — what's wrong?**
Make sure you've added at least one subject with at least one score entered. The AI pulls your current data automatically — if there's nothing in the app yet, it has nothing to work with.

**Q: I forgot my password — how do I reset it?**
On the sign-in screen, click **Forgot password? Reset via email**, enter your email, and you'll get a reset link.
